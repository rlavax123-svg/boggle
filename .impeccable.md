# Design Context

**Target audience**: Casual Boggle players — people who play the physical dice board
game at home with family or partner. Not competitive Scrabble players, not developers.

**Use cases**:
- After shaking the Boggle set, enter the 4×4 (or 5×5 / 6×6) grid and see every word
  you could have made, longest first, with Boggle score.
- Click a word to see its path on the grid so you can verify it.
- Click to see a plain-English definition and a few similar words — learn, don't just
  look it up.

**Brand personality / tone**: Warm, playful but refined — feels like a companion to
a real board game, not a developer tool. Tactile type, inviting colors, confident
but unhurried motion. Avoid: neon-on-black hacker aesthetic, generic tech-dashboard
vibes, purple/cyan gradients.

**Differentiation**: The grid itself is the hero. The tiles should feel like real
Boggle cubes — weighty, slightly shadowed, with legible uppercase characters. Results
feel like a printed word list, not a spreadsheet.

**Technical constraints**:
- Single-page web app, no build step (plain HTML/CSS/JS)
- Must work on phones (one-handed use during a game is common)
- Dictionary (~4 MB) cached to IndexedDB for offline use after first load
